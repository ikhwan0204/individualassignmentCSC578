Ada Lovelace

The world's first computer programmer

Here's a timeline of Ada Lovelace's life:

  • 1815 - Born in London to the poet Lord Byron and Anne Isabella Milbanke.
  • 1833 - Meets Charles Babbage, the "father of the computer," and becomes fascinated by his Difference Engine.
  • 1842 - Translates an article on Babbage's Analytical Engine and adds her own extensive "Notes."
  • 1843 - Her notes describe an algorithm for the Analytical Engine to compute Bernoulli numbers, widely considered the first computer program.
  • 1852 - Dies at the young age of 36.

"That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal; as time will show."

-- Ada Lovelace
